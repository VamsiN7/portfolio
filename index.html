About Me

I'm a software engineer with a curious mind and a passion for understanding the internals of complex systems. Currently pursuing my Master's in Software Engineering at Northeastern University (graduating May 2025), I bring over 3 years of hands-on professional software development experience

            My engineering journey has taken me through the fascinating worlds of fintech and database development. At TCS - Western Union, I architected microservices-based solutions that bridged legacy systems with modern cloud infrastructure, reducing API response times and implementing event streaming that cut incident response time in half. This experience taught me the value of building systems that not only perform well but are resilient and adaptable.

            More recently at AWS, I had the opportunity to dive deep into database internals while building a Data Lake for Aurora's Control Plane. Working with time-series metrics and designing schemas for large-scale analytics challenged me to think at scale, be it optimizing query response times to fetch petabytes of data or creating tools that empowered on-call engineers to gain visibility into metrics that weren't surfaced yet I've cherished working with massive systems that millions of people rely on every day.

I'm driven by a desire for excellence and never settle for "good enough" solutions when better ones are possible. I approach engineering challenges with a blend of technical rigor and creative problem-solving, always maintaining a bias for action that turns complex problems into elegant, efficient solutions.
